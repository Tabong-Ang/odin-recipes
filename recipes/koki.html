Ingredients

  • Banana/Bobolo leaves or Aluminium foil
  • Plantains (Ripe or Unripe)
  • Black-eyed peas
  • Cocoyam leaves
  • Pepper
  • Salt

Preparation Steps

  1. Soak the dry beans in a good quantity of water to soak overnight
  2. The next day, wash the beans by rubbing them between your hands and changing water. When the beans are clean, sieve and put them aside.
  3. Blend the beans and habanero pepper using a blender. Avoid putting a lot of water while blending. Pour the blend into a large bowl.
  4. Warm the palm oil into a pan, just bring it to a lukewarm state.
  5. Mix the blend for a while, then add salt and the lukewarm palm oil progressively. Keep mixing until it is well combined.
  6. Mix the blend for a while, then add salt and the lukewarm palm oil progressively. Keep mixing until it is well combined.
  7. Add that water into the koki mixture and mix. Have a taste to check if the salt is enough, if not adjust.
  8. Pour in a small portion of the koki mixture. Join the ends of the leaves to the centre and tie it with a kitchen string
  9. Pour a cup of water into a big pot, place a leaf at the bottom, then place the bundle into the pot. Let it cook for at least 1 hour. Make sure you check and add water regularly to avoid burns.
